April 2-4, 2004

Spent a bunch of time this weekend shooting sk8 photos.

Mill Creek, Friday night

Payton and I had the evening to ourselves, so we cruised down to the park. We only had about 1/2 hour before bedtime, so there wasn't time for me to skate. I shot a bunch of pix of Matt ("hickskates") and his friend Vern. I hadn't seen Vern skate before - he's one of the most fluid skaters I've seen. A couple of other guys were killing it as well. It was tough to get runs in, as the local kids have taken to using the deep end as a slide. Meanwhile, Payton flirted with a bunch of middle school girl baseball players who were watching from the sidelines.

Bainbridge Island, Saturday Afternoon

I skated Carnation on Saturday morning - had the place to myself for about 2 hours and really pushed myself to try to nail layoffs in the deep end. After some family time, I headed over to Bainbridge island to skate and have dinner with my mom. I skated for about 1/2 hour, and was extremely fatigued - leftovers from my morning at Carnation. I was still having a pretty good day, until my left knee started to twinge. I got off the board and grabbed the cam. Zach, Emil, Dean, and Tom Peha were there, along with some others I didn't know, or know by sight only. The session was inconsistent - bails interspersed with brilliance - and my timing was off, so I got a lot of mediocre shots.

Ballard, Sunday Morning

I hit Ballard for church. Snapped some nice shots of 7-board George and Old Wave Dave, and a couple of TFP Mark. Again, I skated for only a short time - still tired. Although I was fatigued, I felt more in control of my board in the deep than I have in the past - overall, a positive session.

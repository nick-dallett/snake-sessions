Spring trip, 2004

From May 15-June 6, we took a family road trip to San Diego for three weeks. Midpoint of the trip was a conference in San Diego that I attended for work (sadly, I slammed badly at the Clairemont bowl my first day there, so I didn't get to skate that week). Here's the skate highlights of the trip.

Medford, OR

ParisAnne's Nana lives in Medford, so we spent a couple days here on either end of the trip. Medford has a very diverse park. The bowl is big and kinky and I never quite hit my stride there.

Aumsville, OR

I got to Aumsville on the way home after skating all these awesome parks, and I have to say, it is probably still my favorite park ever. IT doesn't look like much, but it's a blast to skate - super smooth, fast, and something to do on every turn. Adam Joslin from Salem was blasting layback airs. Payton was being fussy, so ParisAnne didn't get to snap any decent shots of me, except this brilliant dismount.

Newberg, OR

Newberg is another of the best parks in Oregon. I'd put Newberg, Klamath Falls, Lincoln City II, and Aumsville on the same level. I took a lot of shots of Gar. I met Gar on a previous trip. The dude pretty much lives at Newberg, and he's the air king - these transfers are every bit as insane as they look.

Dave Lawrence came along and tried his hand at skating - he took the pix of me. Before I could get a shot of him on the board, he took a bad spill dropping into the 4' bowl, and required a trip to the ER for stitches.

Fontana, CA

I really like this bowl. It's not huge, and it's pipe coping, but it's super smooth and the tranny is perfect. There was another guy there named Nick who happens to be a skate photographer. He was done for the day, but he snapped a bunch of me. He drove me like a drone, hitting trick after trick until I was completely exhausted. "CLOSER to the HIP!" "YAH! Do it AGAIN!!" "Higher!!"

Chino, CA

Chino's new park has a killer pool. This is like Donald, but with all the kinks removed. Perfect transitions, tile, and pool coping. Shallow end has about 4-6 inches of vert. The first time there, I handed the camera to this 10 year old kid named Kyle and directed him where to stand and when to shoot. He got a killer shot of me grinding the deep. The second time I went there, I ran into Daniel (IronRose) and Dave from the good old PNW. Also there were Eddie and Anton from concrete disciples. Eddie was throwing down killer berts in the deep end. Anton was recuperating from a massive slam at Glendale.

Balboa Creek, Reseda, CA

The day I was going to hit Glendale, we were staying with my friend Frank in Reseda, and ParisAnne came down with a bad headache. We stayed home until she felt better, and by then it was too late to hit the park and still make our evening committments. It was about 100 degrees out, and Frank and I went looking for some banks. We found a killer schoolyard, but we didn't want to barge it, so we spent some time navigating the banks along the bike path at Balboa creek. Frank and I started skating together at 12 years old. Frank hasn't been on a board in years, but since we took these pix, he's called me to say he was ordering himself a new board!

Klamath Falls, OR

I took a day trip from Medford to skate KFalls. What a killer park! I spent most of my time in the Gummi bowl and the square bowl. The anaerobic bowl was too balls-out for my squeamish taste, but the locals were killing it. Katrina Richter, James, Pete, and some kids who ripped (names are on my cheat sheet, which is floating around here somewhere...). James (yellow helmet) snapped the pix of me.

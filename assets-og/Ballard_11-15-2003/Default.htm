Ballard, Tarped - 11-15-2003

I got to Ballard at 8:15 AM, hoping to wait out the rain so I could squeegee. It wasn't letting up. About 8:40, Eric drove by and let me know that Hughes had posted on Sleestak that he was going to come by at 10 to tarp the bowl. I killed some time shooting pix of the graffiti aroud the park. When Hughes arrived, we surveyed the situation. The rain was letting up, so we jumped in with squeegees. We had the bowl almost dry when it started in raining again, so we jumped into tarping mode. Auggie and Old Wave Dave showed up, and the five of us, along with Dave's son Nico and Auggie's lady Amber, took about an hour to tarp the bowl. By the time we got the bowl partially dry, I had to leave to help my mom with a computer problem via remote assistance, but I got a few runs in. The bowl was super-grippy and I was flying. Sadly, I had to leave.

Turns out remote assistance wouldn't work through my mom's proxy, so I was done quickly. Drove back over to Ballard, where the party was in full swing. The bowl was bone-dry, and Morris, Shawn, Skala, Swervo, and Andre had joined the party, along with a few folks I didn't know by name. I dropped in and started throwing down all kinds of stuff I don't normally do - air to fakie, backside airs, handplants - generally kicking it up a notch from my usual poking around. Hughes had his taillights on, and looked like a rocket-powered skater in his Darth helmet in the gloomy interior of the tarp. Swervo was skating super-aggro (dude, he rolled over my toe on a roll-out!), just ripping it up. Move of the day - Swervo doing a backside pivot on the cement-filled tire that we were using to hold up the center support of the tarp.

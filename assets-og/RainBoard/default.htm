My Rain Board

This is the board I was riding in 1989. It's a Z-blank that I cut to a huge fish shape. In 2001, I trimmed it down to a newschool size, and redrilled the truck holes for a more poppy wheelbase.

It's got Tracker fultracks and Santa Cruz Bullets - 97a in back for optimal Ty sliding, and black 92s in front for a little more traction when carving.

It was originally called the "S.S. Fly Bitch", named for the fine honey under the clear grip on top. these days it sports a dogtown cross and goes by the moniker "F.B. Lite"

It's still a sweet board, despite being a bit misshapen. I use it for street carving, rough terrain, and rain skating. The bearings have been resurrected from being frozen solid multiple times - WD-40 does wonders!

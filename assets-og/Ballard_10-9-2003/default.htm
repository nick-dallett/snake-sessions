Ballard Bowl, 10-9-2003

I left work early to go skate Ballard. Got there about 4:00 and it was pouring down rain. I sat in the car for a while, and the rain started to let up. I saw a guy getting ready to jump in and start squeegeeing the bowl, so I grabbed my squeegee and joined him. His name was Wes. Wes and I and a couple of kids named Ryan and Piers got the bowl dry in about 20 minutes.

I proceeded to have my best session ever. Ballard is smooth and grippy right after a wash. wes was tearing the place up. Ryan was hitting some impressive backside grinds at speed. I was feeling really confident in the deep end, flirting with the coping, but still about 10 inches away. I finally started throwing frontside airs in the deep though, at about the vert line.

At about 6:00, it started to rain again so we took a break. Ryan pushed it until he started making tracks. tracks are the warning sign which means it's time to stop, because that's when it starts to get slippery. If you aren't leaving tracks, it means the water is soaking into the surface and not sitting on top.

There was a great rainbow, like a promise from God that we'd skate again that day. Ballard was the pot of gold at the end.

The rain peaked, and Wes jumped in with the squeegee again. Piers jumped in too, sliding down the 9 foot deep end wall on his butt, the crazy kid. They both worked it for a while, and then Ryan and I spelled them. We got the bowl dry again, and started to skate to beat the setting sun. We skated until it was too dark, then said our goodbyes.
